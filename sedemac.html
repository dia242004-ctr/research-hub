The one-liner: A rare Indian company that designs its own control-intensive ECUs in-house and owns a genuinely differentiated technology — sensorless commutation (SLC), which strips the physical sensors out of motors and starter-generators to cut cost and lift reliability. It rides two structural waves (ISG adoption in petrol 2W/3W, and electrification) off a small base — but the book is heavily concentrated (ISG ~64% of sales, TVS ~75%+ of customers) and the stock already trades on tech-leader multiples.

Why Sedemac?

  • A real, ownable technology edge. SLC (proprietary "inductance-tracking" algorithms) eliminates position sensors in motors/magnetos — fewer failure points, lower cost, and it works even in oil-immersed motorcycle magnetos where sensor-based rivals can't. Sedemac claims to be the only player globally to have validated this at scale.
  • In-house design = high entry barrier. Unlike peers who licence or JV for the tech, Sedemac does all design/development itself and keeps full IP ownership — the founders are IIT-Bombay technocrats with a first-to-market track record (smart ignition, sensorless ISG, integrated ISG+EFI).
  • Two structural waves, off a small base. ISG penetration in 2W is ~36% heading to ~50% by FY30, and Sedemac is gaining share within it (~35%→44%+). Separately, EV MCUs are a 43–45% industry CAGR opportunity where its SLC edge repeats.
  • Optionality in large new TAMs. Power tools (US$4–5bn global), e-LCV (validated with Ashok Leyland), and eventually CV/PV — a TAM ladder I-Sec frames as US$125mn → US$12bn, with blue-sky beyond. Not in estimates yet.
  • The catch, stated plainly. Extreme concentration (one product, one customer), import-dependent inputs, and a valuation (81x FY26E P/E) that already prices Sedemac as a tech leader. This is a quality-growth story you pay up for — the debate is diversification and execution.

01

Company overview

A control-electronics tech house sitting between engine/motor makers and the OEM.

Business description

Sedemac designs and manufactures control-intensive, critical-to-the-application electronic control units (ECUs) — the "brains" that govern ignition, fuel injection, starting/generation and motor control in vehicles and gensets. It is one of very few domestic players to develop such control technology fully in-house, retaining complete ownership of design and IP. Its differentiator across products is sensorless commutation (SLC) — proprietary algorithms that remove the physical position sensors rivals depend on.

  • Origins: spun out of a research group at IIT-Bombay; incorporated 2007–08 by four technocrats including Prof. Shashikanth Suryanarayanan. First mass-adopted product was "smart ignition" (2012); sensorless ISG launched 2018; integrated ISG+EFI in 2024.
  • Manufacturing: four units at Chakan, Pune + two technical centres at Baner; ISO 9001 / IATF certified. ISG-equivalent capacity grew 2.47mn (Mar-23) → 3.99mn (Mar-25); utilisation ~76% (FY25) → ~93% (9MFY26).
  • Scale marker: cumulative ISG / ISG+EFI / EFI ECU sales crossed the 10mn-unit mark in 2026.

Industry context & TAM

Two adoption curves — ISG in petrol 2W/3W, and electrification — plus a widening TAM ladder.

The TAM ladder

I-Sec's core framing is that Sedemac has repeatedly grown its addressable market by pushing its control tech into new segments: US$125mn (smart ignition) → US$2bn (genset controllers, sensorless ISG) → US$5bn (EFI, ISG+EFI, MCU/motors for EV) → US$12bn (power tools, e-LCV, ICE-CV) — with a "blue-sky" US$60bn if it ever reaches passenger-vehicle subsystems. Each step reuses the same SLC/in-house-design capability.

Structural tailwinds

  • ISG penetration in domestic 2W is ~36% (FY25) → expected ~50% by FY30 — driven by smoother/silent starts, idle start-stop and fuel-efficiency benefits. ISG market ~₹15.8–16.5bn (FY26E) → ₹30–31bn by FY31 (13–15% value CAGR).
  • Electrification of 2W/3W: domestic EV MCU market seen growing 43–45% (volume) / 44–46% (value) CAGR over FY25–30 to ~₹44–45bn; EV motors 46–48% value CAGR to ₹63–65bn.
  • Emission norms (BS-VI, CPCB IV+): 100% EFI adoption in Indian 2W/3W; electronic governing mandated in gensets — expanding the ECU content per vehicle/engine.
  • New segments: global shift to cordless power tools (BLDC + controllers) is a US$4–5bn MCU opportunity; e-LCV electrification.

Headwinds / what caps it

  • EFI (the 100%-penetrated base) grows slowly — only 2–4% volume CAGR; the growth is in ISG and EV, not the mature core.
  • Import-dependent inputs (semiconductors, PCBs) tie margins to global supply chains and the rupee.
  • Adoption timing risk in EV MCU and power tools — large TAMs, but revenue is still nascent/under validation.
03

The SLC moat

Why "sensorless commutation" is the whole thesis in one idea.

Sensors are the weak point — Sedemac removes them

Motors and magnetos normally need physical (Hall-effect) position sensors to know where the rotor is so the electronics can time the current ("commutation"). Those sensors fail under heat, dust and oil — and add cost and bulk. Sedemac's sensorless commutation infers rotor position purely from proprietary "inductance-tracking" algorithms — no sensor at all. Result: higher reliability (nothing to fail), lower cost, smaller footprint, and — critically — it works in oil-immersed motorcycle magnetos where sensor-based ISG can't go (extending ISG from scooters to motorcycles). Sedemac claims to be the only player globally to have validated SLC at scale.

The same SLC capability travels across products — ISG, MCU for EVs, motors, and power-tool controllers — which is why one core competence keeps opening new TAMs. A second in-house edge: rare-earth-magnet-free motors, reducing dependence on imported critical materials. And by integrating ISG+EFI into one ECU, Sedemac lets OEMs cut part count, cost and packaging space — one of only a few firms worldwide (with Shindengen, Astemo) to offer integrated ISG+EFI, and the only one offering it sensorless.

Business model — the segments

One technology platform, sold into four distinct end-markets. FY25 revenue mix shown.

1. ISG / EFI / ISG+EFI ECUs — ICE 2W/3W ~68% of FY25 rev (~64% 9MFY26)

The core: control units for petrol two- and three-wheelers. ISG lets the magneto act as both starter and generator (silent/quick starts, idle start-stop, better efficiency); EFI governs fuel injection (mandatory post-BS-VI); the integrated ISG+EFI combines both to save OEM cost and space. Sedemac holds ~35%+ share of the ISG ECU market, competing with Shindengen and Denso (who mainly serve Japanese OEMs).

Customers: TVS Motor (lead adopter, 65%+ of its volumes on ISG), Bajaj Auto (Freedom 125, Pulsar N125, 3Ws), Hero MotoCorp (Xoom). Segment revenue CAGR ~25% (FY26E–28E). This is the profit and share-gain engine — but also the concentration risk.

2. Genset controllers & genset EFI ECUs ~12.5% of FY25 rev

Controllers that auto-start gensets on power loss and manage electronic governing (eGov) — Sedemac claims to be the first to integrate eGov within the controller (2014), and is the largest supplier in India (~75–77% share) and top-3 globally. It also supplies genset EFI ECUs (leveraging its 2W EFI scale) as CPCB IV+/Tier-4 norms push electronic fuel systems. Customers: Generac, Cummins, Kirloskar Oil Engines, Mahindra Powerol, DEIF. Domestic market well-penetrated; growth now leans on global share gains (~16% CAGR).

3. Motor Control Units & rare-earth-free motors — EV ~1.5% of FY25 rev · fastest-growing

MCUs control the electric powertrain in e2W/e3W/e-LCV/e-bikes. Sedemac's SLC-based MCU repeats the sensorless advantage; it also makes rare-earth-magnet-free motors (cutting import dependence). E3W OEMs (Bajaj, TVSL) have started using its MCUs; TVSL is validating the E2W version. Off a tiny base, I-Sec models ~86% CAGR (FY26E–28E) — the key diversification lever. Competes with Sterling-GTake, Bosch, Vitesco, Varroc.

4. Newer segments — optionality not yet in estimates

Power tools (US$4–5bn global MCU TAM; in advanced discussions with global players), e-LCV (product validated with Ashok Leyland), and eventually ICE-CV and passenger-vehicle subsystems (blue-sky). These validate the "keep scaling the tech into new TAMs" thesis but are upside, not base-case revenue. Plus ~18% "other products" in the FY25 mix.

The financial shape

Volume/revenue is modelled at ~35%/41% CAGR (FY25–28E); EBITDA margin rises ~260bps to ~21% on operating leverage and richer mix; PAT ~70% CAGR as leverage drops through. Capex intensity is low, so the model throws off strong cash — I-Sec sees ~₹5.8bn OCF and ~₹3.5bn FCF over FY26–28E and a net-cash balance sheet by FY28. Return ratios (RoE) average 30%+.

05

Growth drivers

Penetration × share gain × new segments.
  • ISG penetration ramp — 2W ISG from ~36% to ~50% by FY30, as Bajaj & HMCL follow TVS in adopting it across more models.
  • Share gains within ISG — Sedemac's SLC edge and limited competition (Shindengen/Denso serve Japanese OEMs) take its share from ~35% toward ~44%+ by FY28E.
  • Integrated ISG+EFI — one ECU, fewer parts, lower OEM cost/space → drives both adoption and content per vehicle.
  • EV MCU adoption — already in e3W (Bajaj, TVSL); E2W under TVSL validation — the big medium-term diversifier.
  • New-TAM options — power tools, e-LCV (Ashok Leyland), CV — large addressable markets, upside to estimates.
  • Operating leverage — low capex intensity + rising utilisation (93% at 9MFY26) converts revenue growth into margin and RoE expansion.

07

Key risks

The three the note flags — plus the valuation caveat.
1Product concentration — one segment carries the book

ISG / ISG+EFI ECUs were ~64% of revenue (9MFY26). Sustaining, let alone growing, depends on continued ISG adoption and Sedemac holding its share. SLC-based MCU (EV) adoption is the key diversifier — but it's still early and largely under validation.

2Customer concentration — TVS is the franchise

TVS Motor is ~75%+ of revenue (9MFY26); top-3 customers ~91%, top-10 ~98%+. Diversification hinges on Bajaj/HMCL scaling ISG and on new EV/power-tool customers. Any TVS sourcing shift would be material.

3Import dependence & supplier concentration

Critical inputs — semiconductors and PCBs — are largely imported, exposing Sedemac to global supply chains and currency volatility. Over 63%+ of raw materials come from the top-10 suppliers. Semiconductor price spikes (as in FY22/23) directly hit margins.

4Valuation leaves little margin for error

At ~81x FY26E / ~38x FY28E P/E, the stock already prices in the growth and the diversification. Any slip in ISG penetration, share, or EV/new-segment ramp compresses both earnings and the multiple — the bear case values it at ₹1,075 (~30x a much lower FY28 EPS).

Valuation & verdict

I-Sec: BUY, Mar'27E TP ₹2,350 — 45x Mar'28E EPS (1x PEG on ~46% EPS CAGR).

I-Sec argues that ~30% pre-tax RoCE/RoE, ~46% FY26–28E earnings CAGR and a net-cash balance sheet justify technology-leader multiples. It values Sedemac at 45x Mar'28E EPS of ₹52, giving a base-case ₹2,350 (Mar'27E TP, ~19% upside). The probability-weighted fair value is ₹2,325.

▲ Bull case

  • Ownable tech — only validated sensorless (SLC) player globally; full in-house IP.
  • Twin waves — ISG penetration + electrification, both off small bases.
  • New TAMs — power tools, e-LCV, CV — large upside not in estimates.
  • Quality financials — ~30%+ RoE, ~70% PAT CAGR, net-cash, capex-light.

▼ Bear case

  • Concentration — ISG ~64% of sales; TVS ~75%+ of customers.
  • Diversification unproven — EV MCU / power tools still early/validating.
  • Import + FX risk — semis/PCBs imported; supplier-concentrated.
  • Priced for perfection — ~81x FY26E P/E leaves little error margin.
The verdict (I-Sec's): A genuine tech-moat compounder in a two-wave growth market, bought at tech-leader multiples. The upside is diversification — SLC-based EV MCUs and new TAMs (power tools, e-LCV) converting the one-product, one-customer profile into a broad platform. If that plays out, the premium is earned; if ISG stalls or TVS de-concentrates before EV scales, the valuation unwinds fast. Rating BUY, TP ₹2,350 (~19% upside) — a quality-growth call, not a value one.
